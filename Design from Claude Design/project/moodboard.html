Source · the tree

One tree. Five hundred years. Every piece.

We work with branches and leaves from a single oak in upstate New York. It was a sapling when Magellan crossed the Pacific. It is still standing.

Every plate we make carries the print of one of its branches. The mark is never the same twice — the branch decides.

The ritual of bringing it home

  1. Choose slowly

    Sit with the photographs for an evening. If the piece is still in your mind in the morning, write to us.

  2. We pack it by hand

    Unbleached linen, raw board, no plastic. The packing is part of the work.

  3. Hang it where the light arrives

    Morning light is best. Forget it is there. Remember, one evening, that it is.

Two letters a year. One when the work arrives, one when the tree begins to drop its leaves.
